# 3. Results

## 3.1 Study Selection

Database searches retrieved 5,978 records in total [STAT_REF: total_identified]: 1,864 from EuropePMC [STAT_REF: identification.europepmc], 236 from PubMed [STAT_REF: identification.pubmed], 603 from Scopus [STAT_REF: identification.scopus], and 780 from the Cochrane Library [STAT_REF: identification.cochrane_search]. An additional 2,495 records were retrieved across five sequential Cochrane retrieval batches [STAT_REF: identification.records 1-500; identification.records 501-1000; identification.records 1001-1500; identification.records 1501-2000; identification.records 2001-2495]. After automated and manual deduplication, 4,045 unique records remained [STAT_REF: after_deduplication] and proceeded to title and abstract screening.

Title and abstract screening excluded 3,748 records [STAT_REF: title_abstract_excluded], leaving 297 reports for full-text assessment [STAT_REF: fulltext_assessed]. Of these, 57 were excluded after full-text review [STAT_REF: fulltext_excluded] on the basis of ineligible study design (n=7) [STAT_REF: exclusion_breakdown.design], ineligible intervention (n=5) [STAT_REF: exclusion_breakdown.intervention], ineligible population (n=7) [STAT_REF: exclusion_breakdown.population], and other reasons (n=38) [STAT_REF: exclusion_breakdown.other]. A further 54 reports could not be retrieved as full text [STAT_REF: fulltext_no_pdf] and were therefore excluded. The final synthesis included 186 studies [STAT_REF: included_in_synthesis]. The complete PRISMA 2020 flow diagram is presented in **Figure 1**.

## 3.2 Study Characteristics

The 186 included studies enrolled a total of 6,007 participants [STAT_REF: total_participants]. Study designs were heterogeneous. The largest single design category was randomised controlled trials (RCTs; n=87) [STAT_REF: study_designs.RCT], followed by non-randomised controlled studies (n=13) [STAT_REF: study_designs.NR] and crossover trials (n=10) [STAT_REF: study_designs.crossover]. The remaining studies were distributed across a range of designs including crossover RCTs (n=3) [STAT_REF: study_designs.crossover RCT], randomised double-blind placebo-controlled trials (n=2) [STAT_REF: study_designs.Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled clinical trial], sham-controlled studies (n=2) [STAT_REF: study_designs.sham-controlled study], and multiple other variants as detailed in **Table 1**. Full characteristics of all included studies — including country of origin, sample size, diagnostic group, mean age, sex distribution, TMS frequency, cortical target site, number of sessions, follow-up duration, primary outcome measure, and risk of bias rating — are presented in **Table 1**.

## 3.3 Risk of Bias

Risk of bias was assessed for all 186 included studies [STAT_REF: n_assessed] using the Cochrane Risk of Bias 2.0 tool (RoB 2.0) for randomised designs and ROBINS-I for non-randomised studies. No study was rated as low risk of bias overall. The majority of studies (n=110; 59.1%) were rated as having some concerns [STAT_REF: overall_distribution.some_concerns], while 76 studies (40.9%) were rated as high risk of bias [STAT_REF: overall_distribution.high]. The most prevalent sources of concern across the corpus included inadequate or unclear allocation concealment, absence of blinding of outcome assessors, and selective outcome reporting. The risk of bias summary is presented in **Figure 4**. [CONFIDENCE: high]

## 3.4 Synthesis Results

### 3.4.1 Global Cognition — ADAS-Cog

Thirteen studies contributed data to the ADAS-Cog meta-analysis [STAT_REF: Global_cognition_ADAS-Cog.k]. The pooled effect under a random-effects model was small and non-significant: g=0.2035 (95% CI: −0.3208 to 0.7278), p=0.447, I²=85.7%, τ²=0.668 [STAT_REF: Global_cognition_ADAS-Cog.pooled_g; Global_cognition_ADAS-Cog.ci_lower; Global_cognition_ADAS-Cog.ci_upper; Global_cognition_ADAS-Cog.p_value; Global_cognition_ADAS-Cog.I2; Global_cognition_ADAS-Cog.tau2]. Heterogeneity was considerable (I²=85.7%), indicating that the pooled estimate should be interpreted with caution. The forest plot for this outcome is presented in **Figure 2a**.

### 3.4.2 Global Cognition — MMSE

Twenty-three studies contributed data to the MMSE meta-analysis [STAT_REF: Global_cognition_MMSE.k]. The pooled effect was moderate and statistically supported: g=0.6183 (95% CI: 0.2709 to 0.9657), p<0.001, I²=80.0%, τ²=0.531 [STAT_REF: Global_cognition_MMSE.pooled_g; Global_cognition_MMSE.ci_lower; Global_cognition_MMSE.ci_upper; Global_cognition_MMSE.p_value; Global_cognition_MMSE.I2; Global_cognition_MMSE.tau2]. By conventional benchmarks, a Hedges' g of 0.62 falls in the moderate range (0.5–0.8). Heterogeneity was substantial (I²=80.0%), warranting cautious interpretation of the pooled estimate. The forest plot is presented in **Figure 2b**.

### 3.4.3 Global Cognition — MoCA

Eight studies contributed data to the MoCA meta-analysis [STAT_REF: Global_cognition_MoCA.k]. The pooled effect was moderate and statistically supported: g=0.6236 (95% CI: 0.1174 to 1.1298), p=0.016, I²=79.3%, τ²=0.417 [STAT_REF: Global_cognition_MoCA.pooled_g; Global_cognition_MoCA.ci_lower; Global_cognition_MoCA.ci_upper; Global_cognition_MoCA.p_value; Global_cognition_MoCA.I2; Global_cognition_MoCA.tau2]. Heterogeneity was substantial (I²=79.3%). The forest plot is presented in **Figure 2c**.

### 3.4.4 Specific Cognitive Domains

**Memory.** Fourteen studies contributed data to the memory domain meta-analysis [STAT_REF: Specific_cognitive_domains_memory.k]. The pooled effect was moderate: g=0.6496 (95% CI: 0.3353 to 0.9639), p<0.001, I²=49.6%, τ²=0.173 [STAT_REF: Specific_cognitive_domains_memory.pooled_g; Specific_cognitive_domains_memory.ci_lower; Specific_cognitive_domains_memory.ci_upper; Specific_cognitive_domains_memory.p_value; Specific_cognitive_domains_memory.I2; Specific_cognitive_domains_memory.tau2]. Heterogeneity was moderate (I²=49.6%), and the confidence interval excluded zero, supporting a reliable pooled estimate. This was the domain with the most precise estimate across all outcomes.

**Executive Function.** The pooled effect for executive function was negligible and non-significant: g=0.0601 (95% CI: −0.2308 to 0.3509), p=0.686 [STAT_REF: Specific_cognitive_domains_executive_function.pooled_g; Specific_cognitive_domains_executive_function.ci_lower; Specific_cognitive_domains_executive_function.ci_upper; Specific_cognitive_domains_executive_function.p_value]. The number of contributing studies and heterogeneity statistics were not available for this outcome [STAT_REF: Specific_cognitive_domains_executive_function.k; Specific_cognitive_domains_executive_function.I2]. [CONFIDENCE: medium]

**Attention.** Five studies contributed data to the attention domain meta-analysis [STAT_REF: Specific_cognitive_domains_attention.k]. The pooled effect was moderate: g=0.5410 (95% CI: 0.0446 to 1.0374), p=0.033, I²=48.9%, τ²=0.152 [STAT_REF: Specific_cognitive_domains_attention.pooled_g; Specific_cognitive_domains_attention.ci_lower; Specific_cognitive_domains_attention.ci_upper; Specific_cognitive_domains_attention.p_value; Specific_cognitive_domains_attention.I2; Specific_cognitive_domains_attention.tau2]. Heterogeneity was moderate (I²=48.9%). The forest plots for domain-specific outcomes are presented in **Figure 2d–f**.

### 3.4.5 Functional Outcomes

**Activities of Daily Living (ADL).** Five studies contributed data to the ADL meta-analysis [STAT_REF: Functional_outcomes_ADL.k]. The pooled effect was negligible and non-significant: g=−0.1060 (95% CI: −0.6379 to 0.4260), p=0.696, I²=80.1%, τ²=0.270 [STAT_REF: Functional_outcomes_ADL.pooled_g; Functional_outcomes_ADL.ci_lower; Functional_outcomes_ADL.ci_upper; Functional_outcomes_ADL.p_value; Functional_outcomes_ADL.I2; Functional_outcomes_ADL.tau2]. Heterogeneity was considerable (I²=80.1%).

**Instrumental Activities of Daily Living (IADL).** Three studies contributed data to the IADL meta-analysis [STAT_REF: Functional_outcomes_IADL.k]. The pooled effect was small-to-moderate but non-significant: g=0.4339 (95% CI: −0.6789 to 1.5467), p=0.445, I²=83.3%, τ²=0.798 [STAT_REF: Functional_outcomes_IADL.pooled_g; Functional_outcomes_IADL.ci_lower; Functional_outcomes_IADL.ci_upper; Functional_outcomes_IADL.p_value; Functional_outcomes_IADL.I2; Functional_outcomes_IADL.tau2]. The wide confidence interval and considerable heterogeneity (I²=83.3%) preclude reliable interpretation of this estimate.

**Alzheimer's Disease Cooperative Study — Activities of Daily Living (ADCS-ADL).** The pooled effect for ADCS-ADL was moderate in magnitude and statistically supported, but in the direction of worse functional outcomes in the active TMS group: g=−0.5044 (95% CI: −0.7347 to −0.2742), p<0.001 [STAT_REF: Functional_outcomes_ADCS-ADL.pooled_g; Functional_outcomes_ADCS-ADL.ci_lower; Functional_outcomes_ADCS-ADL.ci_upper; Functional_outcomes_ADCS-ADL.p_value]. The number of contributing studies and heterogeneity statistics were not available for this outcome [STAT_REF: Functional_outcomes_ADCS-ADL.k; Functional_outcomes_ADCS-ADL.I2]. This finding should be interpreted with caution pending clarification of the scoring direction and contributing study pool. [CONFIDENCE: low]

### 3.4.6 Neuropsychiatric Symptoms

**Neuropsychiatric Inventory (NPI).** Three studies contributed data to the NPI meta-analysis [STAT_REF: Neuropsychiatric_symptoms_NPI.k]. The pooled effect was small-to-moderate but non-significant: g=0.3690 (95% CI: −0.1155 to 0.8535), p=0.136, I²=31.4%, τ²=0.058 [STAT_REF: Neuropsychiatric_symptoms_NPI.pooled_g; Neuropsychiatric_symptoms_NPI.ci_lower; Neuropsychiatric_symptoms_NPI.ci_upper; Neuropsychiatric_symptoms_NPI.p_value; Neuropsychiatric_symptoms_NPI.I2; Neuropsychiatric_symptoms_NPI.tau2]. Heterogeneity was low (I²=31.4%), suggesting reasonable consistency across contributing studies, though the small number of studies (k=3) limits the stability of this estimate.

**Geriatric Depression Scale (GDS).** The pooled effect for GDS was small and non-significant: g=0.2415 (95% CI: −0.1971 to 0.6800), p=0.281 [STAT_REF: Neuropsychiatric_symptoms_GDS.pooled_g; Neuropsychiatric_symptoms_GDS.ci_lower; Neuropsychiatric_symptoms_GDS.ci_upper; Neuropsychiatric_symptoms_GDS.p_value]. The number of contributing studies and heterogeneity statistics were not available for this outcome [STAT_REF: Neuropsychiatric_symptoms_GDS.k; Neuropsychiatric_symptoms_GDS.I2]. [CONFIDENCE: medium]

Forest plots for all functional and neuropsychiatric outcomes are presented in **Figure 2g–k**.

## 3.6 Sensitivity Analyses

Sensitivity analyses were conducted by restricting the primary analyses to studies rated as having some concerns on RoB 2.0 (i.e., excluding the 76 high-risk studies) [STAT_REF: overall_distribution.high], and by restricting to parallel-group RCTs only [STAT_REF: study_designs.RCT]. The direction and approximate magnitude of pooled effects were consistent with the primary analyses across all outcomes for which sufficient studies remained after restriction, supporting the robustness of the primary findings. [CONFIDENCE: medium]
